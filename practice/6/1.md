容易有

$$
\overline{X}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^nX_i=158,\overline{Y}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^nY_i=49.78
$$

于是

$$
\hat{\mu}_X=\overline{X}=158,\hat{\mu}_Y=\overline{Y}=49.78
$$

那么

$$
A_{2X}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n\left(X_i-\overline{X}\right)^2=10,A_{2Y}=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n\left(Y_i-\overline{Y}\right)^2=9.95
$$

于是

$$
\hat{\sigma}^2_X=A_{2X}=10,\hat{\sigma}^2_Y=A_{2Y}=9.95
$$
